Privacy Policy

This Privacy Policy describes how the Australian Embassy collects, uses, processes, and discloses your information in conjunction with consular services and embassy operations.

1. Introduction

The Australian Embassy respects your privacy and is committed to protecting your personal information in accordance with the Privacy Act 1988 (Cth) and other applicable laws.

This policy explains how we handle personal information collected through our website, consular services, and other embassy operations.

2. Information We Collect

We collect information necessary to provide consular services and conduct embassy operations. The types of information we collect include:

2.1 Information You Provide

When you use our services, you may provide us with personal information including:

  • Full name, date of birth, and contact details
  • Passport and citizenship information
  • Visa and immigration details
  • Information related to consular cases

2.2 Automatic Information

When you visit our website, we may automatically collect:

  • IP address and device information
  • Browser type and settings
  • Pages visited and time spent
  • Referring website details

3. How We Use Your Information

We use personal information to provide consular services and conduct embassy operations, including:

  • Processing passport and visa applications
  • Providing consular assistance to Australians
  • Communicating about embassy services and events
  • Improving our website and services
  • Complying with legal obligations

4. Information Sharing and Disclosure

We may disclose personal information in limited circumstances:

4.1 With Government Agencies

Information may be shared with other Australian government departments as necessary for official purposes.

4.2 For Legal Reasons

We may disclose information when required by law or to:

  • Comply with legal processes or government requests
  • Protect the rights and safety of individuals
  • Prevent fraud or security issues
  • Address emergencies involving Australians overseas

5. Data Security

We implement appropriate security measures to protect personal information, including:

  • Secure storage systems with restricted access
  • Encryption of sensitive data
  • Regular security assessments
  • Staff training on privacy obligations

6. Your Rights and Choices

You have rights regarding your personal information, including:

  • Accessing your personal information held by us
  • Requesting correction of inaccurate information
  • Making complaints about privacy handling
  • Opting out of non-essential communications

7. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy periodically. The updated version will be published on our website with a new effective date.

We encourage you to review this policy regularly to stay informed about how we protect your information.
