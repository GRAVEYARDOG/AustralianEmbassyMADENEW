Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about consular services, visas, passports, and other embassy matters.

  1. Home
  2. FAQ
01

How do I renew my Australian passport?

Australian passports can be renewed by mail or in person at the embassy. You'll need to complete the application form, provide two passport photos, your current passport, and the applicable fee. Processing typically takes 3-4 weeks. Visit our passport services page for detailed instructions.

02

What should I do if I lose my passport overseas?

If your passport is lost or stolen, contact the nearest Australian embassy or consulate immediately. We can issue an emergency passport to allow you to continue your travels or return to Australia. You should also report the loss to local police and obtain a police report.

03

How long does a visa application take?

Processing times vary depending on the visa type and individual circumstances. Tourist visas are typically processed within 15-20 business days, while work or student visas may take 4-6 weeks. We recommend applying well in advance of your planned travel date.

04

Can the embassy help me find work in Australia?

The embassy does not provide job placement services. For information on working in Australia, visit the Department of Home Affairs website which has comprehensive information on work visas, skills assessments, and employment opportunities.

05

What consular assistance can I receive if arrested overseas?

If you're arrested overseas, we can provide a list of local lawyers, contact your family with your permission, and visit you to check on your welfare. However, we cannot get you out of jail, provide legal advice, or interfere in local judicial processes.
